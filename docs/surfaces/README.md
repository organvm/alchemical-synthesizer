# Surfaces — Product Manifestation Across Domains

> *"All surfaces require surfacing."*
>
> A surface is any face the instrument presents to a world. This directory is
> the register of those faces — internal and external — and the thesis that
> binds them: a system that absorbs identities must itself be *surfaced* into
> the domains it wishes to inhabit.

## The Surfacing Thesis

The Alchemical Synthesizer is built on the premise that nothing has a fixed
identity until it makes *contact*. The same premise governs the product itself:
the Brahma Meta-Rack does not exist in a domain until it is given a surface
there. Code in a repository is *prima materia*; a surface is the transmutation
that lets a particular audience receive it.

We distinguish two classes of surface. Both are required; neither is sufficient
alone.

## Internal ↔ External Coherence

The two classes are not independent. An external surface that contradicts the
internal account is a lie; the discipline here is that **every external claim
must be traceable to [pragma.md](../logos/pragma.md)**. Where the academic or
market surface speaks aspirationally, it inherits the seams pragma names
(placeholder metrics, shallow trait extraction) rather than papering over them.

## Maintenance Contract

- When the instrument changes, update **pragma first**, then propagate to the
  external surfaces — never the reverse.
- New domains (e.g. education, exhibition, open-source community) get a new file
  here and a row in the table above.
- This index is itself a surface; keep it honest.
